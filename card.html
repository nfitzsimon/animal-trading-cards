Sea Otter

Sea otter fur is the densest of any mammal on earth, forming a wetsuit-like layer of protection against cold waters instead of a blubber layer.

  • Scientific Name: Enhydra lutris
  • Average Length: 4.1 ft.
  • Average Lifespan: 15-20 years
  • Habitat: shallow, nearshore waters

Sea otters are playful and intelligent. Being one of the few mammals to use tools, they have pouches of loose skin that allow them to store rocks to crack open hard shells, as well as food for later. They are most commonly found near kelp forests off the coast of the Pacific Ocean.
